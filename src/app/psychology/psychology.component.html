What is Psychology?

Psychology is the art and science of the human mind and the functions of the human mind, especially those affecting behavior in a given circumstance. Psychology is the study of conscious and unconscious elements of the human mind such as thoughts and feelings. Psychology aims to understand the behaviors of individuals and groups through general principles and research specific cases. In all, Psychology is concerned with the complex ways and patterns in which people think, feel, behave, communicate and perceive the world.

how to become a psychologist after 10th If you are interested in pursuing psychology as a career, there are a few steps you can take after completing your 10th grade:

Choose the right stream:

After completing 10th grade, you will have to choose a stream that includes psychology as a subject. Psychology is usually a part of the Arts or Humanities stream. So, you can choose the Arts/Humanities stream for your 11th and 12th grade.

Complete your 12th grade:

Once you have selected the Arts/Humanities stream, complete your 11th and 12th grade with psychology as one of the subjects.

After completing your 12th grade, you can pursue a Bachelor's degree in psychology. You can choose from various options like Bachelor of Arts (BA) in Psychology, Bachelor of Science (BSc) in Psychology, or Bachelor of Education (B.Ed) in Psychology.

Master's degree:

After completing your Bachelor's degree, you can pursue a Master's degree in Psychology to gain in-depth knowledge of the subject.

Internship and Practice:

It is essential to gain practical experience by interning or practicing under the guidance of experienced professionals to gain hands-on experience in the field.

Specialization:

You can specialize in a specific field of psychology like clinical psychology, child psychology, social psychology, or educational psychology.

About psychology

how to become a psychologist after 10th Psychology is the scientific study of the human mind and behavior. It involves examining how people think, feel, and behave, both as individuals and in groups. The field of psychology is diverse and covers various topics such as perception, cognition, memory, emotion, motivation, personality, development, social interaction, and mental health.

Psychology is a broad field, and there are many different subfields within it. Some of the most popular subfields of psychology include:

Clinical psychology:

It focuses on the diagnosis and treatment of mental health disorders.

Counseling psychology:

It involves helping people cope with problems and challenges in their daily lives.

Developmental psychology:

It studies how people develop and change throughout their lives.

Educational psychology:

It examines how people learn and develop within educational settings.

Social psychology:

It studies how people think, feel, and behave in social situations.

Cognitive psychology

It studies mental processes such as perception, attention, memory, and reasoning.
